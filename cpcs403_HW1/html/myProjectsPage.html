This is the home screen of my app. From here, the user chooses whether they want to calculate their GPA for university or for high school. The design is simple, with only two big buttons to avoid confusion.

Once the user selects the University option, they move to this page where they can pick between Semester GPA or Cumulative GPA. I added a friendly academic illustration to match the university theme.

Here, the user selects the grade scale they use. I included three common systems—out of 5, 4, and 100—so the app works for both local and international students.

This is the main input screen where the user adds their courses using the plus button. They can also include their previous GPA and total hours to calculate their cumulative GPA. Once all details are entered, pressing the Calculate button shows accurate overall results.

When the user clicks the plus button, this pop-up appears. They can add a new course by typing its name, credit hours, and grade. The app stores this information for each course and later uses it in the GPA calculation.

After adding all the courses, they appear in a clean list showing the course name, hours, and grade. This helps the user quickly review their input before pressing Calculate to get the final GPA.

If the user tries to calculate the GPA with missing or incorrect values, these messages appear. This prevents wrong results and guides the user to fix inputs.

Once the user finishes adding their courses and hits Calculate, the app shows the final GPA, the equivalent percentage, and whether an honor level or warning applies.
